<h2>About Me</h2>

<p>I'm <strong>A65KJ / NH2OD</strong>, an amateur radio operator originally from Sri Lanka and currently operating from the UAE.</p>

<p>My interest in radio started long before I became licensed. Like many people in the SDR generation, my entry point into the RF world was a cheap RTL-SDR dongle and a lot of curiosity. What began as simple shortwave listening quickly turned into exploring everything I could receive or decode.</p>

<p>Over time that curiosity grew into a wide range of experiments across the radio spectrum. I've worked with weather satellite reception, aircraft and maritime data, amateur radio satellites, and a variety of digital modes. At different times I've experimented with everything from HF digital decoding and ADS-B aircraft tracking to receiving satellite imagery from orbit.</p>

<p>These days I operate mostly from an apartment in the UAE, which means dealing with high urban noise floors and limited antenna space. That constraint has become part of the challenge — figuring out what can still be received and decoded using creative antenna setups and practical hardware.</p>

<p>This blog is a place to document those experiments, share what works (and what doesn't), and explore the radio spectrum one signal at a time.</p>

<p><strong>73 — A65KJ / NH2OD</strong></p>

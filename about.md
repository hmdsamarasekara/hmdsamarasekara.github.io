      <h1>About Noise Floor Nomad</h1>

      <p class="lead">Documenting practical ham radio, SDR, and satellite reception experiments from imperfect setups and real RF environments.</p>

      <p>Welcome to Noise Floor Nomad. This blog is a record of my experiments in amateur radio, software-defined radio, and satellite communications. I focus on practical, real-world setups—not ideal lab conditions, but actual operating environments with all their challenges and surprises.</p>

      <h2>What You'll Find Here</h2>

      <ul>
        <li><strong>Real-world setups</strong> - Equipment that actually works on a budget</li>
        <li><strong>SDR experiments</strong> - From RTL-SDR to more advanced hardware</li>
        <li><strong>Satellite reception</strong> - Weather sats, amateur satellites, and beyond</li>
        <li><strong>Antenna projects</strong> - DIY solutions that don't require a tower</li>
        <li><strong>Signal analysis</strong> - What I find in the spectrum and how I decode it</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Philosophy</h2>

      <p>You don't need perfect equipment or ideal conditions to explore the radio spectrum. Some of the most interesting signals come from improvised setups in challenging environments. This blog celebrates the experimental spirit of amateur radio.</p>

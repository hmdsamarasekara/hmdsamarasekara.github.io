      <h1>About Ground Station Alpha</h1>

      <p class="lead">Exploring the radio spectrum, one satellite at a time.</p>

      <p>Welcome to Ground Station Alpha, a personal blog documenting my journey into the fascinating world of satellite communications and radio monitoring. Here you'll find detailed write-ups of my experiences receiving signals from various satellites, including weather satellites, UHF Satcom, and other orbital transmissions.</p>

      <h2>What I Cover</h2>

      <ul>
        <li><strong>UHF Satcom Piracy</strong> - Monitoring and documenting unauthorized transmissions on military satellite communications</li>
        <li><strong>HRPT Reception</strong> - High Resolution Picture Transmission from weather satellites</li>
        <li><strong>GK-2A & Other GEO Sats</strong> - Receiving imagery from geostationary weather satellites</li>
        <li><strong>Radio Equipment</strong> - Reviews and guides on SDRs, antennas, and signal processing</li>
        <li><strong>Signal Analysis</strong> - Decoding and analyzing various satellite signals</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Equipment</h2>

      <p>My current setup includes various Software Defined Radios (SDRs), custom-built antennas, and signal processing software. I believe in documenting both successes and failures to help others learn from my experiences.</p>

      <h2>Get In Touch</h2>

      <p>Have questions about satellite reception or want to share your own experiences? Feel free to reach out through GitHub or connect with the broader satellite monitoring community.</p>

      <p><em>73s and clear skies!</em></p>

## About Me

I'm **A65KJ / NH2OD**, an amateur radio operator originally from Sri Lanka and currently operating from the UAE.

### Getting Into Radio

My interest in radio started long before I became licensed. Like many people in the SDR generation, my entry point into the RF world was a cheap RTL-SDR dongle and a lot of curiosity.

What began as simple shortwave listening quickly turned into exploring everything I could receive or decode.

### Experimenting Across the Spectrum

Over time that curiosity grew into experiments across the radio spectrum:

- Weather satellite reception  
- Aircraft and maritime signals  
- Amateur radio satellites  
- Digital modes and signal decoding  

At different times I've experimented with everything from HF digital decoding and ADS-B aircraft tracking to receiving satellite imagery from orbit.

### Operating Environment

These days I operate mostly from an apartment in the UAE, dealing with high urban noise floors and limited antenna space.

That constraint has become part of the challenge — figuring out what can still be received and decoded using creative antenna setups and practical hardware.

### This Blog

This blog documents those experiments — what works, what fails, and the occasional strange signal hiding in the noise.

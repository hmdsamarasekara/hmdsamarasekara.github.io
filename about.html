The Operator

I'm A65KJ (also holding NH2OD), an amateur radio operator originally from Sri Lanka and currently operating from the UAE.

My journey into radio wasn't through a traditional ham shack or elaborate antenna farm—it started with a cheap RTL-SDR dongle and an insatiable curiosity about the invisible signals filling the air around me.

From SDR to Licensed Operator

Like many in the SDR generation, I began as a listener. What started as casual shortwave exploration quickly spiraled into late-night sessions decoding everything the spectrum had to offer.

The RTL-SDR was my gateway drug. That tiny USB stick opened doors to:

🛰️ Satellite Reception

Weather satellites, amateur radio satellites, and orbital imagery

✈️ Aviation & Maritime

ADS-B aircraft tracking, ACARS, and maritime AIS signals

📡 Digital Modes

HF digital decoding, PSK31, FT8, and proprietary signal analysis

🔍 Signal Intelligence

Military satellite monitoring, unidentified signals, and spectrum analysis

Each new discovery led to another question. Each decoded signal revealed another layer of the RF ecosystem. Eventually, listening wasn't enough—I needed to transmit, to participate, to join the conversation. That's when I pursued my amateur radio license.

The Challenge: Urban Noise, Limited Space

Today I operate from an apartment in the UAE, facing the classic urban radio operator's dilemma:

High noise floors. Limited antenna space. Restrictive environments.

But constraints breed creativity. Instead of tower-mounted Yagis and acres of land, I've learned to work with:

  • Balcony-mounted antennas disguised as everyday objects
  • Active loops and small magnetic loops for HF
  • Discone antennas tucked into corners
  • Creative grounding solutions in concrete jungles
  • Software-defined filtering to dig signals out of the noise

This blog is a chronicle of those experiments—what works, what fails spectacularly, and the occasional strange signal hiding in the noise.
